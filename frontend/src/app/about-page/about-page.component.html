Why reduce food waste?

Did you know that UK households waste 7.1m tonnes of food per year? On top of this, 5m tonnes of this is edible!!
This have a huge impact on the planet because food waste produces a potent greenhouse gas.

What can I do to reduce my waste?

1. Plan your meals for the week and get more for your money

By planning out each meal of the week, you can prevent over-buying and ensure that every food item get used by the end of the week.

2. Preserve your food to make it last longer

For items you know is about to expire, you can freeze the items for when you are ready to use them.

A common misconception is the 'best before' date. This date simply state that the food item might be best to consumed before that date, but it doesn't necessary mean that the food will go off by that date. Make sure to check if the food is edible even if it is past the best before date.

3. Check if your fridge is at the right temperature

Many food items such as fruit/vegetables can go bad more quickly if the temperature in your fridge is too high. Similarly with meat, fish and poultry, it can go bad if your fridge temperature is too low.
